Introduction

Feral pigeons, also known as rock doves are found across the world. Due to their long history of domestication, they are mostly unafraid of humans. Pigeons live in man-made structures such as buildings, similar to cliffs and rock formations where wild rock doves live. There is much more variation in the colour of plumage in domesticated feral pigeons than their wild rock dove relatives. Urban pigeons tend to have darker plumage than those in more rural areas. In the summer months, feral pigeons may eat grass seeds and berries, but in colder months, they rely on scavenging leftover food waste from humans.

Interesting facts

  • Often thought of as dirty, pigeons are in fact very clean creatures and it is rare that a pigeon will transmit a disease to humans due to their immune system.
  • Pigeons often suffer injuries to their feet and legs due to things such as hair or string becoming wrapped around their legs, sometimes cutting off the blood supply.
  • Some predators of pigeons include falcons, crows, gulls, and owls, as well as raccoons and opossums in North America.
